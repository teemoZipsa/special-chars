💡 What is an IP address?
• IP (Internet Protocol) address is a unique identifier for your device or network on the internet.
• The address shown above is your Public IP, which is visible to the websites you visit, not your local network IP (e.g. 192.168.x.x).
• IPv4 vs IPv6: IPv4 uses dots (e.g. 8.8.8.8) and is limited in supply. IPv6 uses colons and consists of 128 bits to future-proof internet addresses. Your ISP determines which one you are assigned.

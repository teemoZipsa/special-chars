💡 FAQ
Q. What is averaging down vs averaging up?

Averaging down means buying more shares when the price drops to lower your average cost basis. Averaging up means buying more as the price rises to increase your position. Averaging down can reduce your break-even point but increases risk if the price keeps falling.


Q. Why is average cost important?

Your average cost determines your break-even point. If the current market price is above your average cost, you're in profit; below means a loss. By averaging down, you need a smaller price recovery to break even. For example, if you hold 10 shares at $50 and buy 10 more at $40, your new average is $45 — you profit even if the price only recovers to $45.


Q. Is averaging down always a good idea?

No. Averaging down only works if the asset eventually recovers. If the fundamentals are broken, you may be increasing your losses. Always keep position sizing in mind and avoid concentrating too much capital in a single investment.
